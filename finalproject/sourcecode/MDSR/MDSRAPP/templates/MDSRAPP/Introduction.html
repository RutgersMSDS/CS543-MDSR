Milky Way Bulge PSF Photometry Visualization

Introduction

Nowadays with the development of modern industries, human beings are using more and more metal materials. Someday we eventually will use up the world’s resources on the earth. In a quest to solve problems like these, most of us escape into gazing at the sky, turning to the boundless universe. However, as we know, there are countably infinite planets. Traversing all those trillions of celestial bodies will take more than a person’s entire life. So, our spectacular interstellar journey may start with a research to scoop the secrets of the stars at the center of the galaxy, starting with analysis of the huge data (~100 Gb).
This project belongs to CS543: Massive Data Storage and Retrieval developed by Group 1. We provide an interactive map demonstrating the information of more than 600 million stars in the Milky Way which is based on a catalog released by the European Southern Observatory (ESO). The stars are divided into 196 tiles which cover the Galactic bulge by their location in space. Graph waves are also provided. Each star in each tile represents vertices in a graph, and the distance between the stars are calculated to create edge in a graph.
